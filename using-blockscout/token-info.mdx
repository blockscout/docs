## Overview

Token pages on Blockscout can display extra information beyond what's on-chain. This can include project description, website, social links, and a verified icon. To add or update this information, you submit a request that goes through a QA review.

**What you'll need (minimum information required):**

| Field | Required? |
| --- | --- |
| Token name | Required |
| Token contract address | Required |
| Requester name | Required |
| Requester email | Required |
| Project email address (address created with the project's official domain, e.g. `yourusername@yourwebsitedomain.com`, or the official email address shown on the website) | Suggested |
| Project website (working links, clear information) | Suggested |
| Icon URL (48 x 48 SVG or PNG) | Required |
| Project description | Required |
| Social media links | Optional but recommended\* |
| Payment (wallet you are sending funds from and tx hash) | Optional, recommended for expedited processing\*\* |

\*A missing website or social presence won't block approval but does provide relevant context for token info and improves the chances of approval.

\*\*We offer the option to pay for expedited processing of token information. This guarantees we will review and approve or decline the listing within 7 days. If no payment is included we cannot guarantee any timeline for review, as we have 1,000s of tokens in the queue.

### Path 2: You can't submit from your wallet on the Blockscout UI

If the original deployer wallet is not accessible or was never yours (for example after a multisig migration, a treasury wallet change, a team handoff, or a factory contract deployment) you can still submit for verification.

**Steps:**

1. Send an email to [submissions@blockscout.com](mailto:submissions@blockscout.com) with your wallet, token information, and an explanation of your relationship to the project.
2. You will receive a reply with a link to provide additional specific information to the Blockscout team.
3. The team will review the proof and confirms the provided information manually before moving forward.

## Expedited Payment Process

While not required, expedited payment ensures we can review your token info in a timely manner (within 7 days). The payment process varies depending on whether you are following Path 1 or Path 2 submissions.

- Path 1: you will be contacted during the review process if you would like to pay for expedited review.
- Path 2: you will have the option to pay as a part of the during the manual submission.

To initiate the process, send **99 USDC or USDT** to the following wallet on the chain where your token is listed. Current supported networks include **Ethereum, Optimism, Base, and Robinhood Chain**.

## Review Process: what happens after you submit

<Steps>
  <Step title="Wallet verification">
    Path 1 submissions are checked automatically against deployer address/<br /> Path 2 submissions are reviewed manually against the proof you provide.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Duplicate and impersonation check">
    We search for tokens with matching names, symbols, or branding. Tokens may vary slightly; however, if exact duplicates exist, the token with the strongest liquidity, trading volume, and active trader count is treated as official, and copycat tokens are rejected.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Optional website and social review">
    If you include a website or socials, we check for phishing content, suspicious redirects, and impersonation of unrelated projects. This content helps verify correct information, and we recommend you include as much as you can.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Wallet connection review">
    Only if your website has a wallet-connect feature. We test it with a QA wallet holding no valuable assets, checking for unusual permission requests or unlimited spending approvals.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Final decision">
    Approved, rejected, or sent back requesting more information.
  </Step>
</Steps>

**Expedited reviews are completed in 1 to 7 business days**. Standard requests have no guaranteed timeframe.

## Possible outcomes

**Approved** — your token info is confirmed and published shortly after.

**More information requested** — this typically means we couldn't confirm wallet ownership, or something in your submission was incomplete or inconsistent. You'll get a follow-up describing what's needed.

**Rejected** — a rejected submission must restart the full process, including a new \$99 fee if you want expedited processing.

### Common reasons for rejection

- **Payment issues.** The transaction can't be confirmed on-chain, the amount is under \$99, or the transaction hash was already used on another submission.
- **Wallet doesn't qualify.** You couldn't provide sufficient proof of ownership for the token.
- **Token impersonation.** The token exactly copies the name, symbol, and branding of an established token.
- **Phishing behavior.** A linked website contains phishing content or redirects to a suspicious domain.
- **Suspicious wallet permissions.** A linked website's wallet-connect feature requests unlimited spending approvals, unexpected token approvals, or access to unrelated assets.
- **Requests for private keys or seed phrases.** Anywhere in the website or submission flow.
- **Misleading or misrepresented information.** Submitted details are inaccurate or intentionally misleading. Token name, logo, designs, symbol, and other information are fraudulent, infringe copyright, or misrepresent public entities.

<Warning>
  Every submission is final once sent for review. Double-check your token name, contract address, and links before submitting to avoid a rejection.
</Warning>

## FAQ

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Is the $99 fee for expedited review refundable?">
    No. The fee is non-refundable regardless of whether your submission is approved, rejected, or requires resubmission.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Do I need a website or social media to get approved?">
    No. A missing website or social presence doesn't block approval on its own, but is recommended.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Which networks can I pay from?">
    Ethereum, Optimism, Base, and Robinhood Chain. Send 99 USDC or USDT to the address in the [Expedited Payment Process](#expedited-payment-process) section.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="How long does review take?">
    Expedited reviews typically complete in 1 to 7 business days. Standard requests have no guaranteed timeframe.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="What if I no longer control the deployer or first-buyer wallet?">
    Use Path 2. Email our QA team at [submissions@blockscout.com](mailto:submissions@blockscout.com) with proof of your relationship to the project, such as a signed message or a post from the project's verified social account.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Can I submit more than one request for the same token while I wait?">
    No. Duplicate submissions for the same contract address create backlogs and slow down processing. If you have issues with your current request, reply to your original submission thread instead of sending a new one.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Can I edit my submission after sending it?">
    Not immediately. Every submission is final once sent for review. However, once approved, token info can be resubmitted if something needs correcting.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="What happens if my token is a duplicate of an existing one?">
    We compare liquidity, trading volume, active traders, token age, and transaction count. The token with the strongest legitimate activity is treated as official; a newer duplicate attempting to impersonate it will be rejected.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
